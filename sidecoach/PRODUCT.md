# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Design professionals: experienced designers, design engineers, design systems leads, and product designers evaluating new tools for design orchestration and workflow automation. They work in teams, care deeply about quality and consistency, and are looking for intelligent systems that amplify their capabilities rather than replace them.

Context: They spend time researching, implementing, auditing, and iterating on design systems. They encounter bottlenecks in coordination between design research, token extraction, implementation, and validation. They need confidence that their design system stays coherent as teams and products scale.

## Product Purpose

Sidecoach is a design intelligence platform that orchestrates design workflows including research, implementation, and validation into intelligent phases. It detects what designers are trying to do and automatically chains the right flows in sequence, enforcing prerequisites and surfacing regressions.

Instead of scattered commands and manual coordination, Sidecoach sees the whole pipeline: from brand verification through component implementation to accessibility validation. It remembers project context, learns what works, and adapts recommendations.

Success means designers spend less time coordinating flows and more time making decisions. It means design systems stay coherent as products scale. It means quality increases because validation is built in, not bolted on.

## Brand Personality

Smart, confident, bold. Sidecoach owns design intelligence. Not arrogant, deeply knowledgeable. Not cold, helpful and enabling. The personality is that of an expert collaborator: someone who sees patterns the team might miss, asks the right questions, and pushes toward excellence without getting in the way.

Three-word personality: Intelligent, Capable, Enabling

Inspired by Yes& philosophy: "We start with 'yes' and orchestrate what's next." Sidecoach does the same for design workflows.

## Anti-references

Explicitly NOT:
- The tired SaaS dark template (dark blue backgrounds, gradients, hero metrics with big numbers, testimonials that repeat each other)
- Overly trendy design (glassmorphism, blur effects, excessive animations that distract from content)
- Minimalist corporate (single accent color, everything flat, playing it safe) that loses personality
- AI-slop patterns (gradient text, identical card grids repeating endlessly, side-stripe borders as accents)
- Generic "design tool" aesthetic that every design orchestration tool uses

## Design Principles

1. **Show Don't Tell** - The landing page should demonstrate Sidecoach's intelligence through its own structure and clarity, not through testimonials or marketing speak. Architecture sections show how the system works.

2. **Confidence Through Clarity** - Use bold typography, generous spacing, and decisive color choices. Design professionals respect clarity over decoration. Every element serves a purpose.

3. **Pattern as Proof** - Sidecoach detects patterns and chains workflows. The page should surface these patterns: how flows connect, how phases build, how validation flows through layers.

4. **Respect the Craft** - Design professionals know good design. Speak their language with precision. Avoid hype; focus on capabilities and architecture.

5. **Yes& Momentum** - Borrowed from the inspiration: "We start with 'yes' and orchestrate what's next." Pages should feel forward-moving, positive, building on capability rather than hedging uncertainty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA standard as baseline
- High contrast for readability (especially important for long-form content; cap line length at 65-75ch)
- Semantic HTML structure for screen reader navigation
- No color-only information conveyance
- Reduced motion support: respect prefers-reduced-motion and provide static alternatives
- Keyboard navigation fully supported
