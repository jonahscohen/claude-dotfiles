# PRODUCT.md

## Register

**Mixed**

## Primary Users

Two distinct audiences served by a single site: (1) potential collaborators evaluating the studio's taste and identity work via the marketing surface, and (2) existing clients who log in to a working dashboard that lists active engagements, invoices, and shared files. The marketing pages must look like a brand site; the dashboard must look like a product.

## Brand Personality

Marketing pages: editorial and confident, voice of a senior designer.
Dashboard: workmanlike and terse, voice of a tool you trust.

The site is a test case for whether one design system can serve both registers without one cannibalizing the other.

## Anti-References

- Marketing-style hero gradients leaking into the dashboard
- Dashboard density patterns appearing on the marketing pages
- A "brand-y" sign-in form that hides where to type
- Editorial typography on a data table

## Strategic Principles

- Two reading surfaces, one token system
- Typography axis bends register: display serif on marketing, mono numerics in dashboard
- Color reused across both, but tinted differently on raised surfaces
- Motion is generous on marketing, almost absent in dashboard
- No marketing copy inside an authenticated view
